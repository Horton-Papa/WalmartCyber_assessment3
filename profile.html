Background

I have somewhat of a non-traditional path into the technology field. After high-school, I decided to work immediately, and I spent about 10 years of my life working in the automotive industry: first as a technician, and later on as a service advisor (the guy you go "see" when you service your car)

I really enjoyed what I did, albeit, it was extremely stressful and fast paced: angry customers, happy customers, demanding management, chill managers, etc.

However, there was always something I thought to myself, "I wonder what it would be like to go to college?" So I did. I went to community college, and started school, with my regular 9-5. This journey was quite a ride!

...but that part is a story for a different day

Fast forward six years, an undergrad in applied math, and a MS in Statistics, I can say, now I know what it was like to go to college.

What's next for me?

Currently I am in the Data Science field, and I am always trying to improve and explore what else is possible. Being in this bootcamp allows me to not only learn new skills, but to also learn more about myself.

I would be lying if I said I know exactly where I am going, but the truth is I don't know, and that is better than I could have ever hoped for. I just enjoy the challenges, try to do my best and enjoy the ride!
